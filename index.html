*About me

I am a Machine Learning Engineer, a math major, and a dyslexic. I have been writing code for about 11 years (on and off), primarily using MATLAB and C++ to explore mathematical concepts such as plotting fractals like Julia and Mandelbrot sets, as well as developing functions to aid in my coursework.

Then I learnt Python, and my range expanded significantly. I have since worked on various projects encompassing Object Detection and Recognition, Time Series Analysis, Sentiment Analysis, and Reinforcement Learning. My passion lies in the realm of AI, where I find delight in using Python to explore its potential. Additionally, I actively contribute to open-source libraries during my free time.

Beyond tech, I spend my time in the world of physics, often reading articles and watching videos related to the subject, particularly in the those related to subatomic particles and quantum mechanics. I have a keen interest in conducting research in Quantum Computing within a Math and CS PhD program. I hold a keen interest in fields such as real analysis, topology, differential equations, and complex analysis.

Although my primary research interests are still evolving, I find joy in studying an array of topics ranging from Quantum Chromodynamics to Prime Number Theory and AI ethics.

My favorite differential equation is a simple linear second-order ODE, and My favourite equation is the Euler-Lagrange equation from Variational Calculus.

In my leisure time, I indulge in playing chess, contributing to Wikipedia articles, exploring world-building games (Cities Skylines is my fave), and supporting Manchester United.
